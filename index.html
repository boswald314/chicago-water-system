The full cycle: Lake Michigan → intake cribs → purification → pumping stations → 5 million taps → combined sewers → interceptors & Deep Tunnel → treatment plants → the reversed river system → the Mississippi. Everything above the purple band is the drinking-water system (City of Chicago Dept. of Water Management); the purple band is TARP, 150–300 ft underground; treatment and waterways belong to the MWRD and partner agencies.

Toggle extreme-storm mode to see what happens when the tunnels and reservoirs fill: pumping stations and outfalls discharge combined sewage to the rivers, and in the worst storms the lakefront gates open and the system deliberately "re-reverses" into Lake Michigan.
